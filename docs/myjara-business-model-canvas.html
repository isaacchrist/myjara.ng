MyJara — Business Model Canvas

How governed jara turns retention into revenue

Nine blocks, grounded in what's actually built (docs/ROADMAP.md) and what's next — not an aspirational rewrite.

Abuja, Nigeria
17 July 2026
Live today Next / planned

Key Partners

  • Flutterwave — card, transfer, and mobile money processing
  • Abuja market associations & LGA contacts across AMAC, Bwari, Gwagwalada, Kuje, Abaji, Kwali — ground-level trust for market-day trader onboarding
  • Early wholesaler/brand partners — first sellers to operate under a governed jara standard
  • NEXTLogistics/delivery partners — not yet scoped

Key Activities

  • Building and enforcing the Jara Standard — structured value fields, verification gates
  • Operating four seller-format dashboards: physical shop, online store, market trader, wholesaler/brand
  • Store verification and dispute resolution
  • LGA-by-LGA rollout across Abuja

Key Resources

  • The Jara Standard engine — buy-X-get-Y logic and substitute-value fields (core IP)
  • Multi-tenant marketplace platform with row-level-security data isolation
  • Flutterwave payment integration
  • Founding team — Isaac-Christ Ikpe, Damilola Thompson

Value Propositions

  • Buyers: jara you can trust — a structured, real bonus, not a marketing claim
  • Sellers: retain customers without burning margin on discounts or ad spend
  • Wholesalers/retailers: compete on differentiated value, not price wars — choose what extra value looks like, prove it was given
  • Everyone: trust built into the platform — verified stores, real disputes, secured personal data
  • Built for every seller format Nigerian commerce actually uses, not one generic template

Customer Relationships

  • Self-service dashboards per seller format
  • Buyer-seller chat tied to orders and products
  • Admin-mediated dispute resolution
  • Community trust via familiar market-day traders
  • NEXTPremium relationship tier for buyers

Channels

  • myjara.com.ng — direct platform
  • Custom domains for wholesaler/brand stores
  • On-the-ground onboarding in physical markets
  • Word of mouth and local market reputation
  • NEXTSeller-funded ads for in-marketplace visibility

Customer Segments

  • Buyers — Abuja shoppers seeking verifiable value, not just low prices
  • Physical shop owners
  • Online store owners
  • Market-day traders
  • Wholesalers / brand sellers — highest-value, most differentiated segment

Cost Structure

  • Platform engineering and hosting
  • Flutterwave transaction and processing fees
  • Store verification and dispute-resolution operations
  • Market-by-market growth and onboarding costs across Abuja LGAs
  • NEXTAds-platform infrastructure and wholesaler-billing engine development

Revenue Streams

  • LIVESeller subscriptions — admin-editable pricing per shop type
  • NEXTPer-conversion wholesaler billing — pay-per-completed-sale, replacing flat tiers
  • NEXTSeller-funded ads — paid visibility inside the marketplace
  • NEXTPremium buyer tier — paid access layer for buyers
