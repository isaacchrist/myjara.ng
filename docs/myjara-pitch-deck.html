01 — The Name

Jara isn't a feature. It's a habit we're digitizing.

In Nigerian markets, jara is the little extra a trader throws in after you've paid — a few more tomatoes, an extra wrap of soap, a gesture that costs the seller almost nothing and buys loyalty no discount code can. It's older than e-commerce, and better at retention.

MyJara builds that gesture into the transaction itself. Not a marketing label — every order line item on the platform carries an actual bonus quantity alongside what was paid for.

03 — The Solution

One marketplace, four kinds of sellers, one bonus culture holding it together.

MyJara is a multi-tenant marketplace built for how Nigerian commerce actually works: physical shops, online-only stores, market-day traders, and wholesale brands, each with their own dashboard — connected to buyers through search, direct chat, and the jara they already expect from a seller worth trusting.

06 — Trust & Safety

Trust isn't a badge. It's enforced in the schema.

  • Every store is verified before it goes live. New stores sit in a pending queue and become active only on a human admin decision — not an autosign-up.
  • Disputes have a structured pipeline. Buyers and sellers resolve problems through a real dispute process with admin resolution, not a review that just makes the seller disappear.
  • Chat is tied to the order, not a phone number in a bio. Conversations reference the actual product or order, so context isn't lost off-platform.
  • Personal data is locked down by default. Row-level security ensures a customer's own bank details and home address are visible only to them — enforced at the database, not just the UI.

08 — The Jara Standard

Jara isn't optional marketing copy. It's a governed commitment — built to become a verified one.

Any seller can claim they offer value. MyJara is built to make that claim checkable: every product carries a structured jara field, not a description a seller writes and nobody confirms. A wholesaler selling phones can jara the same phone, or choose a different form of equivalent value — the platform's job is to make "equivalent" mean something, not let the word do the work discounts and ad spend used to.

Live
A structured jara field on every product — a buy-X-get-Y bonus quantity, computed straight into cart, checkout, and order totals, not a promotional label.
Live
Sellers already choose the value type — more of the same product, or a named, described, imaged substitute item of the seller's choosing.
Next
Making jara a requirement, not a default of zero — today a listing can go live with no jara at all; the governance version of this ties jara presence into store/listing verification, the same review queue that already gates a store going live.
Next
Equivalence checked, not self-reported — for categories like phones and other high-value goods, defining what counts as an equivalent substitute (so "extra value" can't be a token gesture) is the next scoping question, the same category of decision as the wholesaler billing mechanics.

This is the platform's central differentiator, not a side feature — everything else (verification, disputes, analytics) exists to make jara a claim the platform can stand behind.

10 — The B2B Engine

Brands get a storefront and a growth number, not just a bulk order sheet.

  • A dedicated brand dashboard, separate from retail sellers, built around bulk relationships instead of single-item listings.
  • A subscription tier priced for wholesale, and adjustable by the platform without a redeploy.
  • Next: billing tied to actual conversions. Moving from a flat monthly fee toward paying per completed sale — brands pay for growth, not for a login.

11 — Where We Start

Abuja first, market by market.

Nigeria's retail economy is still overwhelmingly built around physical and open-air markets, even as mobile internet use keeps growing — that gap between how people actually shop and what's actually online is the opportunity.

MyJara launches area by area across Abuja's own local government areas — AMAC, Bwari, Gwagwalada, Kuje, Abaji, and Kwali — mapping the marketplace onto markets that already exist, instead of asking sellers to abandon them for a generic national listing.

Directional market context, not sourced figures — firm up with real market-sizing data before external investor use.

12 — How MyJara Makes Money

Three layers, one live, two sequenced on purpose.

Live
Seller subscriptions — priced differently by format (physical, online, market-day, wholesale), fully admin-adjustable.
Next
Per-conversion wholesaler billing — replacing the flat wholesale tier with a rate tied to completed sales.
Next
Seller-funded ads — paid visibility for sellers who want more reach than organic discovery gives them.
Next
A premium buyer tier — a paid layer for buyers who want more than the default marketplace experience.

Not built until the mechanics are right: exact conversion definition, rate, and collection method are deliberate open decisions, not oversights.
